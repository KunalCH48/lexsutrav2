## What LexSutra Is

LexSutra is an **EU AI Act compliance diagnostic platform** — a professional service
with a technology platform, not a SaaS tool.

The best analogy: **a professional house inspection, but for AI systems.**
- A house inspector doesn't guarantee the house is safe — they identify what's wrong
- LexSutra doesn't certify compliance — it identifies gaps and provides a roadmap
- The inspection report is the product. The report IS what clients pay for.

**Core product:** A graded PDF diagnostic report — colour-coded by obligation,
legally cited against specific EU AI Act articles, version-stamped, human-reviewed,
with a prioritised remediation roadmap.

**Key differentiator:** Every diagnostic includes mandatory human expert review
before delivery. AI drafts the findings. A qualified human validates every clause.
This is non-negotiable and is the USP.

## The EU AI Act — Key Facts

- Full name: **Regulation (EU) 2024/1689** of the European Parliament
- High-risk AI compliance deadline: **August 2, 2026**
- High-risk categories (Annex III): employment/HR, credit/finance, education,
  healthcare, critical infrastructure, law enforcement, migration, access to services
- Two key roles: **Provider** (builds/sells the AI) and **Deployer** (uses it)
- Providers and Deployers have different obligations — we assess both
- The regulation is real, in force, and enforcement begins after the deadline

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Overstating the free assessment** — it is indicative only, based on public info,
   AI-generated without human review. Never imply it's equivalent to a full diagnostic.

2. **Using "certify" or "validate"** — we assess and report. We do not certify.
   Certification implies an authority we don't have or claim.

3. **Implying full automation** — the human review step is what makes this premium.
   Always mention it. "AI-assisted with human expert review" — not "automated."

4. **Quoting prices** — pricing is quote-based and context-dependent. Direct people
   to request a quote. Never state a fixed number in public-facing content.

5. **Saying clients will be "compliant"** — we identify gaps and give them a roadmap.
   What they do with it is up to them. Compliance is their responsibility.

6. **Mentioning B.V. or VAT** — company not yet registered. Keep it to "LexSutra,
   based in the Netherlands."

7. **Describing LexSutra as a legal service** — we are a compliance infrastructure
   tool. Clients should engage qualified legal counsel for legal decisions.

8. **Claiming the public footprint scan is comprehensive** — it reads publicly
   available website content only. It cannot access internal systems, code, or data.
